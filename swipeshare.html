A SOLUTION TO SHARE MEAL SWIPES ON THE GEORGIA TECH CAMPUS

In the Fall 2017 semester, our team found that many Georgia Tech students had an immense surplus of meal swipes towards the end of the semester with the inability to utilize all their swipes. Many of these students would share their extra swipes with their friends by lending them their buzzcard (student card). This matter intrigued our team to explore easier ways to share meal swipes.

*Meal swipes can purchase a value of $10 at any dining area on campus. They are a part of prepaid meal plans, and cannot be carried over to the next semester.

MEAL PLANS ARE NOT REALISTIC

Here's what we found in our research (Interview, Online Surveys, and Observation):

  • Meal plans assume that youâ€™ll eat on campus dining areas 3 times a day for each day of the week. This is not realistic because some students eat out off campus, go home on weekends, get free meals from clubs etc
  • Several students were posting on social media pleading for other students to use their meal swipes so they would not go to waste.
  • Students with meal swipes mostly share with friends; Survey showed most students would not mind sharing with strangers, but wouldn't go out of their way to share with strangers
  • No real benefit for sharer
  • Physically have to be with the person to share or give your student card to the other person
  • No central communication on social media

DESIGN CRITERIA

Social:

Be more than just a share-and-go but to provide a space for socializing and networking

Convenient:

Should be easy and quick to use

Safe:

Transparency between the sharer and receiver

DESIGN IDEAS

The Winner?? Location Check-In

A mobile application would be used so that a meal swipe sharer can check-in at one of these specified spots on campus (West Village, Bottom floor of SC, other dining halls). If a user is checked in, another user in the same dining area can make a request for a meal swipe from the sharer. The sharer can accept or deny the request. If accepted, the two users can then message each other to coordinate the transaction.

USER TESTING

We conducted Task Analysis and Think-Aloud testing, with follow-up interviews. Here's some feedback & suggestions we received:

  • Too many buttons on the landing screen
  • No confirmation from both people to share swipes
  • Application does not inform which dining hall is selected
  • Give users preferences for who to match with
  • Verify that users are at the correct location

FINAL DESIGN

No More Messaging

"Hey I'm sitting close to Chick-fil-a"
"Ok, I'll be there soon."
The conversation is so minimal, so why add a messaging feature? In this iteration, the sharer gives a short description of where they are and/or of themselves. The receiver should be capable to locate the sharer based on the location and description.

Swipe Shared Button

In the previous design iteration, the Swipe Shared button was located in an awkward spot. Testers felt the urge to press it because it was towards the bottom, close to the send message button. Some were also confused about the functionality of the button. The new design makes it clear when the user should drag the switch, and since it is a switch, it would be difficult to accidentally drag the button.
